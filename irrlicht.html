IRRLICHT

Installation - 2021

Irrlicht explores the concept of ghosts as hyperobjectsâ€”entities that transcend time and space, existing everywhere yet remaining unnoticed. Inspired by Timothy Morton's Hyperobjects, I draw a parallel between the elusive nature of ghosts and the ambiguity of what is human and non-human.

The work incorporates a distance sensor that interacts with images and sound. When a viewer aligns with the sensor, the visuals and audio slow down and eventually pause. Once the viewer moves away, the image reactivates, continuously fading until it becomes unrecognizable. This dynamic behavior mirrors the way ghosts seem to appear, linger, and disappear unpredictably in three-dimensional space.
